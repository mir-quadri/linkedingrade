§ 01 — THE MARK

Two shapes. Two colors. One idea.

A navy rounded square anchored by a red block in the bottom-right corner. The red block's outer corner inherits the parent's radius so the seal reads as a single object, not two stacked rectangles. The negative space in the upper-left does as much work as the fill. Mondrian-clean, Chase-tier construction.

Construction

Grid100 × 100 unit canvas. The red block occupies the bottom-right 36 × 36u — 36% of the linear edge, the visual golden mean for corner stamps.
ContainerRounded square, r = 6u. Slightly more radius than v2's r = 4 because the corner block needs the rounded edge to read as a unified seal, not a sticker.
Red blockSolid #C8102E rectangle from (64, 64) to (100, 100). Outer bottom-right corner radius matches the parent at r = 6u. Top and left edges are flat.
Why bottom-rightAnchors weight to the lower-right, which is where the eye naturally rests when scanning Western-reading content. Same posture as a signature, a wax seal, a printer's mark on the colophon.
Clear spaceMinimum 0.5× the mark's height of clear space on all sides. Don't put it inside another container without breathing room.
Min. size14px for screen, 8mm for print. The red block stays visible because it's 36% of the linear dimension — well above the 25% threshold for legibility at micro size.

§ 05 — SPEC

Two colors. One font.

The whole identity, hand-it-to-the-printer page. Navy, red, Geist. That's the system. Anything more is decoration.

COLOR

NAVY · #0F2138 ACCENT · #C8102E WHITE · #FFFFFF

Navy is the body. White is the surface and the type. Red is the corner block — and only the corner block. Never use red as a fill larger than 5% of the composition. Never use red on body text. Never use red on a button background.

TYPE

WORDMARK · GEIST 700 · −0.035em SUPPORT · GEIST MONO 500 · UC · +0.08em

Both faces ship via Google Fonts. The mark resolves to a single SVG under 300 bytes — no font dependency, no rasterization issues, no hinting headaches. Embeddable in email signatures.

CLEAR SPACE & PROPORTIONS

Mark height equals 1.2× the wordmark's cap height in horizontal lockup. The gap between mark and wordmark equals 0.6× cap height. Minimum clear space around any lockup equals 1× cap height on all four sides. Don't crowd it — the negative space is what makes the corner block read as a corner block and not a misprint.

§ 06 — DON'TS

Six ways to break it.

The brand survives if these six rules survive. Every one of them is something a well-meaning vendor, agency, or eager intern will try at some point. Print this section and tape it to the wall.

× DON'T ADD LETTERS The mark is geometric. No initials, no monogram, no "G", no wordmark inside. Letters belong beside the mark, never inside it.
× DON'T RECOLOR No teal, no purple, no LinkedIn blue. Navy + accent red + white. Three colors, full stop. Brand audits the world; the world doesn't audit the brand.
× DON'T ROTATE The corner block sits in the bottom-right. Always. Rotating the mark moves the block to the wrong corner and breaks the visual signature.
× DON'T ROUND IT Radius stays at 6u — the seal is a square with softened corners, not a pill, not a circle. Circles signal "app," squares signal "stamp."
× DON'T ADD SHADOW No drop shadow, no glow, no bevel, no gradient. The mark is two flat shapes. Depth is the SaaS-template move we built this brand to avoid.
× DON'T MOVE THE BLOCK Bottom-right corner is the signature. Top-left is a different brand. Top-right is a different brand. The block lives at (64, 64) to (100, 100).
— WHY THIS WINS

The case for the Stamp.

Zero collision risk. No letters means no trademark, copyright, or monogram conflict with anyone. LG, GE, Google, Goldman — all share initial letters with us; none of them share a shape. Two rectangles arranged in a specific way is an extremely defensible mark.

It reads as the product. The product stamps profiles with a grade. The mark looks like a stamp. The corner block reads as "approved" the way a postage cancellation or a quality seal does. The visual logic and the product logic are the same logic.

It scales further than a monogram. A 14px favicon of LG loses its kerning, a 14px favicon of G loses its terminus character, a 14px favicon of the Stamp loses nothing — the corner block stays 36% of the linear dimension regardless of size. The mark is its own worst case.

It is a name, finished. LinkedInGrade, set in Geist 700, anchored by the Stamp. Ready for the printer, the embroiderer, and the engineer.
